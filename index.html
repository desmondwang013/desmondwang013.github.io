About Me

Hi! I am a graduate student in Management & Analytics at New York University, focusing on machine learning, data mining, and applied modeling. I'm currently a PhD applicant for ML and Data Science programs.

Thanks to advice from Dr. Andrew Banasiewicz and Dr. Omar Alvarez-Pousa, I am able to explore stacked ensemble methods and spatiotemporal feature engineering for wildfire susceptibility prediction in California.

My broader interests include reinforcement learning, deep reinforcement learning, and trustworthy AI — especially algorithmic calibration, risk estimation, and confidence modeling in data-efficient systems. During my undergraduate study, I researched media-driven stock market dynamics, working with Dr. Chen Yi. And with Dr. Antoine Jacquier on empirical modeling and human–machine hybrid prediction methods. Outside research, I enjoy creating animations and concept models in Blender.
